Overview

At NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory, I worked on the High Contrast Imaging Testbed to enable picometer-scale stability in spaceflight coronagraph hardware by characterizing deformable mirror (DM) surface deformation under controlled temperature drift. :contentReference[oaicite:17]{index=17}

What I Did

  • Designed a vacuum-compatible thermal control system with PID-regulated heaters and four-wire sensors.
  • Automated 12-hour thermal test cycles to sweep PID gain parameters.
  • Quantified DM surface displacement using a Twyman–Green interferometer and Zernike decomposition in Python. :contentReference[oaicite:18]{index=18}
